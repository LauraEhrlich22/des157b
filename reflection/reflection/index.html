Reflection

A lot of the article 30 Years on from Introduction the Web to the World talked about how much the web has grown since it's creation. I always think it is interesting to think about how people can be so skeptical of inventions that end up being thought of as almost a necessity today. This is especially true with the invention and creation of the internet and web. If you were to then go and ask many gen-zers today if they can imagine a world without the web, I honestly think a lot of them having grown up with having answers in your fingertips for more than half of their life, would answer, no, they can't.

With this all being said, as an individual there are countless website, proejcts, and whatever else you find on the internet that have definitely been impactful to me. However, I think one of the first things that comes to mind would be a simulator that simulates part of the rare neurological visual processing disorder that I have. With my condition, I think there is still so much uncertainty with it, that a lot of people feel like they are crazy, often being accused of doing drugs and having that be the cause. I remember when I was first diagnosed, or first realized what Visual Snow Syndrome (the condition I have) is it was so hard to find any answers on it since it was before there was really any sort of establishment out there for it. I think after a year of searching the web, I eventually came accross this website that gave me a way to begin to start modeling what I was seeing. I remember feeling a sense of relief of wow! There are others out there too experiencing this, it's a real thing and no I'm not crazy. So, in other words, despite the controversies that come with the internet, there is most definitely good that can come with it.
